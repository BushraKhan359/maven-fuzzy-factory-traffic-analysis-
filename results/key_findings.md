# Key Findings — Maven Fuzzy Factory Traffic & Conversion Analysis

## Finding 1 — Gsearch Is the Backbone of the Business
Gsearch nonbrand drove the majority of sessions and orders throughout 2012. The business is heavily dependent on this single channel, which represents both a strength (it's working) and a risk (any disruption to Gsearch would significantly impact revenue).

## Finding 2 — Desktop Converts Far Better Than Mobile
Desktop sessions converted at roughly 4–5x the rate of mobile sessions. Despite mobile driving meaningful traffic volume, it generates very few orders. This points to a significant mobile UX problem worth investigating and fixing.

## Finding 3 — The Lander-1 Test Generated ~50 Extra Orders Per Month
Replacing the default homepage with a custom landing page improved conversion rate by 0.87 percentage points. Over 22,972 sessions since the test, this translated to approximately 202 incremental orders — roughly 50 extra orders every month at no additional ad spend.

## Finding 4 — The Billing Page Test Added ~$10,153 in Monthly Revenue
The new billing-2 page generated $8.51 more revenue per session than the original billing page. With 1,193 billing sessions in the past month, this represents approximately $10,153 in incremental monthly revenue from a single page redesign.

## Finding 5 — Brand and Organic Traffic Are Growing
Over time, brand search, direct type-in, and organic sessions grew as a percentage of paid nonbrand traffic. This is a strong positive signal — the business is building real brand equity that doesn't require paid spend to sustain.

## Recommendations
1. **Fix mobile UX** — The gap between desktop and mobile conversion is too large to ignore. A mobile-specific landing page or checkout flow redesign could unlock significant revenue.
2. **Diversify traffic channels** — Heavy dependence on Gsearch nonbrand is a risk. Investing in content marketing, SEO, and email would reduce this dependency over time.
3. **Continue A/B testing** — Both the lander and billing tests delivered measurable revenue lifts. A culture of continuous testing is clearly working and should be expanded to other funnel pages.
